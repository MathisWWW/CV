Research Projects

Projects at Institute of Metal Research, CAS

Heat-treatment & water vapor corrosion behaviors of rare-earth silicates environmental barrier coatings. Shenyang, China

Role: lead researcher Sept. 2023 – July. 2024

  • Raw powders of rare-earth silicates coatings were synthesized, corresponding topcoat are fabricated using the air plasma spray (APS) method and subsequently heat-treated, and finally underwent multiple water vapor corrosion tests at elevated temperature.

  • Extensive characterization was conducted using SEM(SE/BSE)-EDS, XRD, FIB-TEM techniques to investigate the effect of heat-treatment and water vapor corrosion on the coatings’ properties.

  • Further quantitative analysis was performed on the extracted microscopic images to quantify the coatings’ properties. The evolution of cracks, porosity and TGO growth were studied.

Oxidation behavior of HEAs under static & cyclic high-temperature conditions. Shenyang, China

Role: lead researcher Nov. 2023 – July. 2024

  • Four typical HEAs were prepared and static/cyclic oxidation tests were carried out. Surface & cross-section SEM-EDS, oxidation kinetics, and XRD analysis were performed.

  • The effect of alloying elements and cyclic condition on the HT performance of alloys was studied.

  1. Keyu Wang, Ziyu Wang, Tiefeng Du, Yang Cui, Zhixin Luo, Yixiu Luo, Jing Liu, Luchao Sun, Jingyang Wang. (2025). An in-depth exploration of microstructural characteristics and phase evolution in RE-silicates (RE= Er0.25Tm0.25Yb0.25Lu0.25) multiphase environmental barrier coatings utilizing digital image processing techniques. Journal of the European Ceramic Society, Volume 45, Issue 8, doi: 10.1016/j.jeurceramsoc.2025.117276 [BibTex] [Link]

    • Multiphase high-entropy rare-earth silicate environmental barrier coatings (EBCs) were processed using APS method with specifically designed feedstocks.

    • Phase distribution and evolution, crack characteristic and porosity feature within the coatings were quantitatively analyzed using digital image processing techniques, and underscore the pivotal role of feedstock composition in controlling structural integrity of EBCs.

    • Disilicate-rich coatings, including M25D75, M10D90, and D100, exhibit superior cross-sectional morphology characterized by a significantly reduced number of cracks, as confirmed by quantitative analysis. These coatings show promising potential for EBC applications.
